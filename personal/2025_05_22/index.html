Why not 100% stocks?

Investing in bonds offers stability, income, and diversification, making them a core component of many portfolios—especially for risk-conscious investors or those nearing retirement.

11 / 28

Bonds

  • A bond is a type of loan made by an investor to a borrower—typically a corporation, municipality, or government. In return for the loan, the issuer agrees to:
  • Pay periodic interest (called the coupon), and
  • Repay the principal (the original loan amount, or face/par value) at a specified future date (maturity).
  • A bond is a fixed-income instrument!

Equities

Equities are financial instruments that represent ownership in a company. When you own equity in a company, you own a piece of that business and are entitled to a share of its profits (usually in the form of dividends) and potential capital gains if the company's value increases.

23 / 28

Equities Offer Strong Long-Term Growth

  • Historical data shows equities outperform bonds and cash over long horizons
  • Investors are compensated for taking on more volatility and risk
  • Growth potential makes equities a core part of most portfolios
  • Reads:
  • Fama, E. F. (1965). The behavior of stock-market prices
  • Markowitz, H. (1952). Portfolio Selection
24 / 28

Diversification Enhances Risk-Adjusted Returns

  • Combining uncorrelated assets reduces portfolio risk
  • Enables more consistent performance even in downturns
  • Foundation of Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT)
  • Reads:
  • Markowitz, H. (1952). Portfolio Selection

Passive Investing Has Proven Reliable

  • Index funds consistently outperform most active managers after fees
  • Low-cost, diversified exposure to the entire market
  • Ideal for long-term investors seeking consistent participation in equity growth
