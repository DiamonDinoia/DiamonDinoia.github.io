Definitions

Investing: A long-term strategy of purchasing assets — such as stocks or real estate — and holding them over time, regardless of short-term market fluctuations, with the goal of building wealth gradually.

Trading: The buying and selling of financial assets like stocks, currencies, or commodities, typically with the goal of making short-term profits from price changes.

Part 3 / 3 — Funds

Funds

Index funds, mutual funds, ETFs — and a look at why fees and incentives matter.

Funds

Index Fund: A type of investment fund designed to track the performance of a specific market index, such as the S&P 500. It’s passively managed, typically has low fees, and aims to mirror the returns of the index it follows.

Mutual Fund: An investment fund that pools money from many investors to buy a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities. It is actively or passively managed by professionals, and investors buy shares in the fund rather than owning the underlying assets directly.

ETF

A wrapper that trades like a stock, holds a basket like a fund.

ETF

Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF): A type of investment fund that holds a collection of assets — like stocks or bonds — and is traded on stock exchanges, just like individual stocks. ETFs aim to track the performance of an index or sector and offer diversification, low fees, and liquidity.
